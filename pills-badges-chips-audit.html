The confirmed bug: filter pills rendering as badges

In the sidebar, Priority and Platform filter pills are built as class="b-plat fbar-pill". .b-plat hardcodes border-radius:4px; .fbar-pill never overrides it. So even though they behave exactly like pills (click to toggle, opacity/glow states, live count), they render with sharp badge-like corners. Play-status filter pills don't have this bug — see below, side by side.

Open decisions before I change anything

  • What should the one canonical pill radius be, used everywhere (selector pills AND filter pills)? Today it's split between 20px (most modal selectors) and 99px (play-status, and most badges/chips). Option A: 99px everywhere (matches the majority of the app's rounded elements) · Option B: 20px everywhere (matches most modal selector buttons today)
  • .plat-tab (purchase-editor platform switcher) — restyle as a selector pill to match platform elsewhere, or leave as its own "tab" pattern since it's arguably a different job (switching between existing records, not assigning a value)?
  • .fbar-hot-chip (hotness tier filter) — rename/restyle as a filter pill (add the count, match the other sidebar filters), since it behaves identically to them?
  • .cich / .cich-ro / .cich-tag (genre/dev/publisher/collection tag inputs in Add/Edit modal) — restyle to match .fchip's chip look for consistency, or are form tag-inputs legitimately allowed to look different from the sidebar's active-filter chip?
  • .sc-chip (aggregate stats readout) — in scope for this pass at all? If yes, does it become a 4th category ("stat"), or do you want it folded into badge/chip?
  • Calendar (.cal-count, .cal-chip, .cal-tba-chip) — confirm out of scope, or fold in?
